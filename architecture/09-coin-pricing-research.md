### 2.4 "Impulse Buy" Threshold
Based on the price anchors:
- **Under 500 MNT** (~$0.14): "no thought" purchase (less than a bus ride)
- **500-1,000 MNT** (~$0.14-$0.29): true "impulse buy" territory (a fraction of daily spending)
- **1,000-2,000 MNT** (~$0.29-$0.58): comparable to 1 day of mobile data; most users would still consider this low
- **2,000-5,000 MNT** (~$0.58-$1.45): "small purchase" -- equivalent to a snack or drink; requires minor deliberation
- **5,000-10,000 MNT** (~$1.45-$2.90): approaching coffee/small meal territory; noticeable spend

**Recommendation: Target 300-1,000 MNT per episode as the sweet spot for impulse-level pricing.**

- **SocialPay**: 93% of Golomt Bank transactions are online

- This means **frictionless micropayments are already normalized** in Mongolia -- a major advantage for MNREELS

## 3. Coin Package Psychology

### 3.1 Why Coins Instead of Direct Pricing?

**Psychological distance from real money:**
Virtual currencies create a buffer between spending real money and consuming content. When a user sees "10 coins" instead of "500 MNT," the pain of paying is psychologically reduced. Research confirms this leads to higher and more frequent spending.

**Key psychological mechanisms:**
1. **Decoupling effect**: The purchase of coins (real money event) is separated in time from the spending of coins (consumption event). The "pain of paying" happens once during the top-up, not on every video watched.
2. **Denomination effect**: Small coin numbers feel trivial. "3 coins for an episode" feels negligible even if 3 coins cost 500 MNT.
3. **Sunk cost fallacy**: Once users have loaded coins, they feel compelled to use them rather than let them "go to waste," leading to continued engagement.
4. **Anchoring to virtual value**: Users anchor their value perception to the coin denomination rather than the real-money equivalent, making repeated purchases feel natural.
5. **Remainder coins trap**: Packages are designed so users always have "leftover" coins that aren't quite enough for one more purchase, motivating another top-up.

### 3.2 Optimal Number of Package Tiers

Industry best practice uses **4-6 tiers** to serve different user segments:

| Tier Purpose | Target User | Typical Range (USD equiv.) |
|-------------|------------|--------------------------|
| **Starter/Trial** | First-time buyer; wants to test | $0.50-$1.00 |
| **Casual** | Light user; watches 1-3 videos/week | $1.50-$3.00 |
| **Regular** | Engaged user; watches daily | $5.00-$10.00 |
| **Value** | Power user; binge viewer | $15.00-$25.00 |
| **Super** (optional) | Superfan; also gifts creators | $40.00+ |

**Why this structure works:**
- **3 tiers is too few**: Misses the trial buyer and the superfan
- **7+ tiers is too many**: Creates decision paralysis
- **4-5 tiers is optimal**: Covers all user segments while maintaining clear differentiation
- **The middle tier should be the "intended" choice**: Users naturally gravitate toward the middle option (Goldilocks effect)

### 3.3 Discount Structures That Drive Larger Purchases

| Discount Strategy | How It Works | Typical Discount |
|------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Volume discount** | More coins = lower price per coin | 5% (small) to 25% (largest) |
| **Bonus coins** | "Buy 100, get 10 free!" | 5-15% bonus at higher tiers |
| **First-purchase bonus** | Double coins on first purchase | 100% bonus (one-time) |
| **Limited-time offers** | Flash sales on specific packages | 20-30% extra coins for 24-48 hours |
| **Loyalty tiers** | Cumulative purchase rewards | 5-10% permanent bonus after N purchases |

**Best practices:**
- Show the "savings" amount prominently on each package (e.g., "Save 20%!")
- Mark the most popular/best-value package with a badge ("BEST VALUE" / "MOST POPULAR")
- Use **bonus coins** rather than discounted prices -- "100+15 Bonus" feels like getting something free, while "15% off" feels like a transaction
- Never offer a discount greater than ~30% -- it devalues the currency and trains users to wait for sales

### 3.4 "Bonus Coins" Strategy Deep Dive

The most successful platforms (WEBTOON, TikTok, Webnovel) all use bonus coins rather than price reductions:

**WEBTOON example:**
- $9.99 = 100 coins + 10 bonus (10% bonus)
- $19.99 = 200 coins + 25 bonus (12.5% bonus)
- $49.99 = 500 coins + 75 bonus (15% bonus)

**Why bonus coins work better than discounts:**
1. Users perceive "getting more" as more rewarding than "paying less"
2. Bonus coins don't change the displayed price-per-coin ratio, preserving price integrity
3. Users feel they have "extra" to spend, leading to trying content they otherwise wouldn't
4. Bonus coins can expire (creating urgency) while base coins don't

  - 1 тасалбар = 50₮ → 2 тасалбар = 100₮ ($0.029) — PPP sweet spot-оос ч доогуур, маш хүртээмжтэй

## 5. Summary & Key Principles

1. **Price for Mongolia, not the US**: International platforms charge $0.30-$1.00/episode targeting $5,000/month incomes. MNREELS targets ~$406/month incomes (1,400,000₮). Тасалбар бүр 50₮, анги бүр 2 тасалбар = 100₮ — маш хүртээмжтэй.

2. **Автобусны билетээс хямд**: Анги бүр 100₮ (2 тасалбар), автобусны билет 500₮. Impulse buy мэдрэмж бүрэн хангана.

3. **Тасалбар ашиглах, шууд мөнгө биш**: Сэтгэл зүйн decoupling нь зарцуулалтыг 20-40% нэмэгдүүлдэг. "Тасалбар" нэршил нь хуулийн эрсдэл бууруулна.

4. **Бонус тасалбар > Хөнгөлөлт**: "15 бонус тасалбар авах" нь "13% хямдрал"-аас илүү сэдэлтэй.

5. **Дунд багц = anchor**: Дунд багц (5,000₮) = хамгийн тодорхой "ХАМГИЙН АШИГТАЙ" сонголт. QPay-д тохиромжтой дугуй тоо.

6. **First episode free, always**: This is the single most important conversion tool. If the content is good, users will pay for episode 2.

7. **Remainder coins drive re-purchase**: Package sizes (30, 65, 135, 290, 600) are intentionally designed so that after watching a series, users have a few coins left over that aren't quite enough for the next video, prompting another package purchase.

8. **QPay/SocialPay integration is critical**: With 3.2M QPay users and normalized QR payments, the purchase flow must be seamless -- ideally 2 taps from "buy coins" to "coins loaded."
